Introduction to Taxes

Taxes are the mandatory contributions individuals and businesses make to the government to fund essential services such as education, healthcare, infrastructure, and public safety. Understanding how taxes work helps you make smarter financial decisions and avoid unnecessary financial strain.

Why You Should Understand Taxes

  • To plan your income and spending better.
  • To know your rights and benefits as a taxpayer.
  • To avoid penalties or legal trouble from missed or incorrect payments.
  • To take advantage of tax incentives and deductions available to you.

Common Types of Taxes

  • Income Tax: A percentage of your income paid to the government. Rates vary depending on your income level and country.
  • Value Added Tax (VAT): A tax added to the sale of goods and services, often paid by the final consumer.
  • Property Tax: Based on the value of property or land you own. Often used to fund local governments.
  • Capital Gains Tax: Paid when you profit from selling assets such as stocks, real estate, or a business.
  • Corporate Tax: Paid by companies based on their profits.
